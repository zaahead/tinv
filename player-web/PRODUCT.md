# tinv — Product Context

## Register
product

## Product Purpose
tinv is a **video converter and player**. It compresses ordinary videos into
tiny `.tinv` files (AV1/Opus in an obfuscated container only tinv can decode),
and plays them back. The core benefit is **small file size**: a video becomes a
fraction of its original size while staying watchable. The PWA converts short
clips in-browser and plays any `.tinv` (local or a shared link) with zero
install; the desktop CLI handles large files fast.

## Users
- **Students / viewers** opening a shared lecture link on a laptop or phone.
  They want it to just play, full keyboard control, resume where they left off.
  Not technical. They never installed anything.
- **Teachers / creators** who occasionally convert a short screen recording in
  the browser when they don't want the desktop CLI.

## Brand & Tone
Quiet, precise, technical-but-calm. The tool disappears so the video is the
focus. No marketing gloss, no playful mascots. It should feel like a
well-made instrument — closer to a pro video tool or a code editor than a
consumer media app. Confident minimalism, not sterile emptiness.

## Hard Constraints (non-negotiable, from the owner)
- **Strictly black and white / monochrome.** No color accents. Grayscale only.
- **No rounded corners anywhere.** Everything square (0 radius).
- **Modern, good-looking, not bland.** Minimal but crafted, with real
  hierarchy and rhythm — not a flat gray void.

## Anti-references (what to avoid)
- Generic "dark SaaS dashboard" look (cards everywhere, soft shadows, blue accent).
- Consumer media-player chrome (YouTube/Vimeo gloss, rounded pills, brand red).
- AI-slop minimalism: centered everything, one font weight, no rhythm, no detail.
- Glassmorphism, gradients, neon.

## Strategic Principles
- The video is the hero; controls and chrome recede until needed.
- Density and precision over whitespace-padding. Information-rich but calm.
- Monochrome forces hierarchy through type, weight, spacing, and contrast steps
  rather than color. Lean into that as the aesthetic, not a limitation.
- Every surface earns its keep. No decorative containers.
